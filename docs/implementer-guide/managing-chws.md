# Managing CHWs

This guide covers how to add community health workers, assign supervisors, and manage CHW accounts through the admin dashboard.

## Adding a New CHW

1. In the admin dashboard, click **Users** in the left sidebar.
2. Click **Create User**.
3. Fill in the form:
   - **Username** -- a short, unique login name (e.g., `chw.liu`)
   - **Real Name** -- the CHW's full name
   - **Password** -- set a temporary password
   - **Role** -- select **CHW**
4. Assign the CHW to the correct **Project**.
5. Click **Save**.

Share the username and temporary password with the CHW. They will use these credentials to sign in to the mobile app and should change the password after their first login.

## Assigning Supervisors

Supervisors monitor CHW activity and visit completion. To set up supervision:

1. Create a user account with the **Supervisor** role (see the same process as creating a CHW above, but select "Supervisor" as the role).
2. Assign the supervisor to the same project as the CHWs they will oversee.
3. Supervisors can then view visit reports and CHW activity for their project through the admin dashboard.

## Viewing CHW Activity

To see a CHW's activity:

1. Click **Users** in the left sidebar.
2. Find the CHW in the user list. You can filter by role or search by name.
3. Click on the CHW's name to view their profile and activity.

From here you can see:
- Which families are assigned to this CHW
- Visit completion rates
- Recent visit activity

## Assigning Families to CHWs

Families are typically assigned to CHWs through the mobile app during the initial registration visit. However, admins can also manage assignments from the dashboard:

1. Click **Babies** in the left sidebar.
2. Find the family record.
3. View or change the assigned CHW.

## Deactivating a CHW Account

If a CHW leaves the program:

1. Go to **Users** and find the CHW's account.
2. You can delete the account or reassign their families to another CHW first.
3. Reassign any pending visits to the new CHW.

Always reassign families before deactivating a CHW to ensure continuity of care.

## Bulk Operations

For programs with many CHWs, consider:

- Creating accounts in batches during onboarding events
- Using a standard naming convention for usernames (e.g., `chw.firstname.lastname`)
- Preparing a printed list of usernames and passwords to distribute during training

## Tips

- **One CHW per device.** The mobile app is designed for one CHW to be signed in per device.
- **Regular password changes.** Encourage CHWs to change their passwords periodically.
- **Monitor login activity.** If a CHW has not logged in recently, they may need technical support or a password reset.
- **Supervisor oversight.** Assign supervisors to review visit quality, not just completion rates.
